Eatogether

A social dining application for students to eat together and make friends

Individual Project | Introduction to Interaction Design

Interaction Design, Wireframing

Introduction

University students love food and making new friends. The cultrual diversity on American campus also makes it possible for students to taste food from different regions and learn more about a new culture. However, they lack a platform to connect with each other.

In the class of Introduction to Interaction Design, I designed a social dining application to help students connect with each other by having casual dinner/lunch together.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed the pros and cons of existing offline events and some Social Dining Applications which enable people to eat with strangers or share food with strangers.

Ideation

I brainstormed and sketched 8 ideas and their storyboards, and picked up 2 which might be final solutions potentially.

  • Users post their home-made food on an app and find somebody to eat together casually.
  • Users post their home-made food and make friends on an app, and organize potluck offline with the app.

User Research

I interviewed 14 students in different years and from various schools and created personas.

  • They prefer to eat lunch/supper with a small group of people casually rather than organize potlucks, which is time-consuming.
  • They don't mind eating with strangers who are also from U-M, but they hope they can invite a friend to join.
  • They hope to eat with someone who shares similar food styles or interests.
  • They prefer to join a table rather than send a request to a stranger to eat together.

Design Rationale

Based on the interview, I decided the final solution, a social dining app that helps students eat together in a small group casually during lunch/dinner hour.

I conducted QOC (Questions, Options, Criteria) analysis to find the best approach to the key features and created wireframes for alternatives.

  • How users schedule a time and place to eat together?
  • How users view each other's food and find a friend?
  • How users contact each other and form a group?

Paper Prototype

I created and tested the paper prototype and revised it based on the users' feedbacks.

  • Set the schedule page as the homepage.
  • Add "return" and "done" in several interactions.
  • Simplify the page of joining a table, and reduce redundancy.

Design (Round 1)

I created the digital prototype with Sketch and Invision.

Find friends based on users mutual schedules
  • Users can make a schedule by entering a location and choosing a time slot during lunch/dinner hour.
  • The time slot is fixed, such as from 12:00 pm to 1:00 pm and from 1:00 pm to 2:00 pm, to make the scheduling process easier and increase the possibility of finding someone who will eat in the same time slot.
Eat with a friend who shares food styles or interest with a user
  • Users input their food styles and interest in their profile. They will also tag the food style when they upload their food.
  • When users view a list of other users, the system prioritizes those who share similar food styles or interests with them.
Join/create a table and dine in a small group with strangers or friends
  • Users can create or join a table which provides 5 seats at most so users can eat in a small group casually.
  • Users can invite a friend to join their table to avoid eating with all strangers.

Design (Round 2)

This is my first project of interaction design. After I gained more experience in interaction design, I am now revising the design based on the feedback I got in the past one year.

The new design will focus on:

  • Streamline the scheduling process
    • Use a map to show the existing tables or active users on campus to help users find a table more easily
    • Use a time picker instead of the calendar to make the rule of scheduling more understandable
  • Improve the interaction of joining and creating a table
  • Provide a system for the table owner to accept or reject participants
  • Provide a system for participants to rate each other to make sure users can meet nice people with nice food
  • Make the product more visually attractive

The new design will come soon!
