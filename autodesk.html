Autodesk FormIt 360

An architectural modeling software

Class Project of Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation

Key words: UX Research

Introduction

Autodesk FormIt 360 is an architectural modeling softwarefor architects to sketch, collaborate, analyze, revise early-stage design concepts.

In the class of Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation, I worked in a team of 4 to conduct user research on Autodesk FormIt 360 for our client, Autodesk.

Interaction Map

Interview

We conducted interviews with 3 architecture graduate students and 1 former architect and created personas.

  • Different types of users have different software preference.
  • Users want to be able to transition from one software to another with ease.
  • A trade-off exists between the easiness of use and the accuracy of functions.
  • When it comes to putting the final details on a project, many users run into difficulties.

Comparative Analysis

We compared 8 direct, indirect, and parallel comparators in 10 dimensions.

Survey

We implemented a survey and received 126 responses from architecture professionals and students. 59% of the survey takers had experiences with FormIt 360. Some key findings include:

  • Formit 360, Revit, and AutoCAD are top performers in icon discoverability, clean interface, system precision, and collaboration.
  • Compared with clean UI, precision is more important for users.
  • Sketching in 3D on a mobile app is not regarded as important as we expected.
  • Supporting integration with Revit to connect design concepts to BIM without remodeling is the most important factor for users to choose a new 3D modeling software and use FormIt 360.
  • Users do not have a strong motivation to use 3D modeling software on IOS.

Method

We created 10 criteria and conducted heuristics evaluation based on Nielsenâ€™s usability heuristics.

We conducted 5 usability tests with Master architecture students who never have experiences with FormIt 360. We created 3 tasks and observed how they completed the tasks to understand:

  • What is the first experience of the product like? Can they perform a set of basic modeling tasks?
  • Can they find the tools needed for the task at hand? If not, where do they struggle?
