AllergyBot

A Chatbot for young adults with food allergies finding restaurants

UX Research | UX Design

Introduction

15 million Americans have food allergies. More and more young people are suffering from food allergies with the number of allergic children growing.

We found that fear of getting a reaction when dining out impairs their social aspect of life and the exhausting process of searching and communicating also increase their anxiety.

Thus, we designed a humane chatbot that provides restaurants’ allergy accommodation information based on users’ allergens on the instant messaging platforms young adults are familiar with.

This project was selected as the finalist (Top 12) in CHI 2017 Student Design Competition.

My Role

In a team of 5, I worked as the user researcher, UX designer and video producer.

Approach

We adopted the user-centered design and tried to get users and stakeholders involved in the whole process.

Research

Understand Target Group

Method

To understand the daily life and challenges of young people with chronic illness, we did:

  • A questionnaire survey with 168 respondents from the target group
  • 5 user interviews
  • 3 expert interviews
  • 20 restaurant interviews
  • 16 literature review
Findings
  • For people with food allergies, dining-out causes most convenience and risks.

Survey result: Under what circumstances do you think food allergy cause inconvenience?

  • Food allergic young adults have the most social needs but have the highest rate of fatal allergic reaction.
  • Young people with food allergies are exhausted by the complex searching process of finding allergy-educated restaurants.

Interviewee 1:"I usually spend 2 hours to find a restaurant where I can eat. I google to see if there is any recommended restaurant. I sometimes use Allergyeats, but there is not enough review on it. "

  • They do not want to cause inconvenience to their friends because of their allergies.

Interviewee 2: "I don't want to be the burden of my friends. I use Facebook Messenger to chat with my friends about restaurants, but if I find the restaurant is not allergy-educated by after the conversation, I will not tell his them."

  • The difficulties in dining out influence their social life negatively.

Interviewee 3: "Sometimes I have to find an excuse to leave if the restaurant is not allergy-educated."

  • Most restaurants lack the motivation to advertise their allergy-friendly service.

Interviewee (restaurant owner): "We provide good accommodation for allergic people, but I don't want to advertise this. They are a small portion of the customers, and the accommodation is additional work for us."

Understand the market and policy

We researched the existing designs and solutions from the following 3 aspects.

  • Food detecting devices: Allergy Amulet is a portable and food allergen detection device. For users who have several allergies, they need to buy several devices.
  • Restaurants recommendation platform: AllergyEats helps people with food allergies to find restaurants. However, the number of reviews is limited.
  • Legislation: It would be ideal to enact laws to improve food allergy awareness in restaurants, but the implementation is difficult.

Definition

Based on the findings above, we defined the problem we want to solve as:

How to help young people with food allergies find a restaurant more easily and better communicate with their friends and restaurant about their concern?

Ideation

Exploration

We had the following ideas in the first-round of ideation.

  • A game that educates young adults to avoid risks in real life.
  • An application which provides users with easy choices of dining-out.
  • A plug-in built in messaging tools that help users communicate their concerns with friends.
  • A platform where restaurants can appeal their allergy-educated service with coupons.

The interviews with restaurants made us realize that the idea of "a platform for restaurants to advertise their service" will be hard to implement without the support of restaurants.

Decision

After further research on technology, we reconsidered the idea of "a plug-in built in messaging tools" and decided to build a chatbot.

  • It is built in existing instant messaging apps or platforms such as Messenger and uses existing restaurants APIs.
  • It allows users to access information about restaurants' accommodation conveniently in a familiar manner.
  • Users can check a restaurant quickly and tell their friends their concern during the conversation naturally, thus reducing the inquiry workload and the sense of “a burden to others”.

Wireframing

We discussed the following questions when creating the wireframes:

  • In what possible ways does a user want to check a restaurant?
  • What is the logic user flow to check and review a restaurant?
  • What and how many options should the Chatbot prompt to the users?
  • How does the Chatbot collect the users' information, inside of the conversation or through a form?
  • What is the best way to rate a restaurant?

We created first-version wireframes on the whiteboard and created the paper prototype for the usability test.

Usability Test

We tested low-fidelity prototype with 3 users and improved the design based on the following findings:

  • Users hoped to have additional functions, like the price of restaurants, and bookmark of the restaurants they are interested in.
  • Users were confused about how the system reminds them to write a review.
  • Users wanted shorter text and hoped to tap the restaurant image to see basic information.

User Flow

Final Design

Personalized Allergy ID

  • Users can input their personal allergy information, so AllergyBot can provide information related to the user’s allergies.
  • Users can also share this allergy information in a form of card to their friends or the restaurants.

Check a restaurant and see allergy-free menus

  • Users can check the allergy accommodation of a specific restaurant or a restaurant nearby, view special menus that exclude their specific allergens, and call the restaurant.
  • Users can save and share the restaurants and menus they are interested in.

Contribute reviews and make AllergyBot smarter

  • AllergyBot detects users’ locations and asks the users to provide allergy-related reviews after they visited a restaurant they saved
  • AllergyBot can get smarter with more users’ search history and reviews, and become more customized to users’ needs.

Final Design Overview

Create and share personal allergy information

Check a restaurant and see individualized allergy-free menus

Receive a reminder to write reviews

Take-aways

Design should start from a problem and provide a solution. In this project, after we decided to target the people with food allergies, we had no clear idea what we should design because we had not found the problem we want to solve Ideas jumped out more easily after we did the research and clarified the problem and users' needs.
