Mint Redesign

A Personal Finance & Money app that helps keep track of spending and stick to budgets

Mobile App | Product Design | Interaction Design | UX Research

Intro

As a Mint user, I can always feel some interactions cumbersome or some features insufficient when I use it. After reading some reviews on Apple store and Mint blog, I found that I am not alone.

This motivated me to do a design exercise to redesign Mint in my free time. Here is what I did within one week:

Challenge

Unhappy voices from Mint users
  • ”Swiping card” makes it hard to find crucial information

    • ”In exchange for a customizable overview of all of your financial data at a glance, there are loads of ‘smart insights’ to sift through to find crucial budget information – why those numerous swipes from the lunch screen are baffling.”

  • Budgeting feature in this budgeting app is not powerful

    • “The budgeting tool is only good for retroactive reviews rather than planning ahead.”

    “If you can include a smart budgeting feature that is centered around savings goals, that would be big.”

How might we help users understand finances easily and manage a budget effectively to support their financial success on Mint?

Solution Overview

The new Mint brings flexibility and efficiency in viewing financial information, and more importantly, provides smart budgeting, personalized financial advice, and reflection on spending behavior to help a user manage their money better.

View the data you care most in the customizable dashboard

Budget holistically and smartly for income, spending, and savings goal

Gain rich insight, reflect on your spending, and get actionable tips

Research

People use personal finance apps frequently, anytime, anywhere, and within a short time frame.

“I am using Mint every day whenever I have time.”

“The last time when I used it was a few minutes ago when you called me.”

  • How often do they use a personal finance app?

    • 48%    Every day

    44%    At least once a month

  • How much time do they spend on this app when using it?

    • 70%    Less than 3min

Getting insights and budgeting are the 2 major use cases for a personal finance app.

“I want to know where my money is gone so that I don’t need to be anxious.”

“I used Mint mostly to see whether my spending is over budget”

  • What is your major purpose of using a personal finance app?

    • 88%   See where my money has gone

    44%    At least once a month

  • To what extent do you evaluate the importance of the features?

    • 80%    Rated “budget planning and budget status” as important and very important

Persona

Design

Goal 1: Help users understand their finance at a glance
Problem

“Swiping” makes users unable to find the most important information quickly

  • The interaction of swiping across cards is cumbersome

    • “I need to swipe 4 times to find the card of ‘budgets’, which is the one I used most frequently. I cannot remember where it is.”

  • Users need to view some information together

    • “I cannot correlate my budget with my spending by swiping.”

  • Different users have different priorities when viewing their financial information

    • “I’d like to have a customizable dashboard like what my school financial department is providing.”

Before: Current Mint

Solution

View important information in the customizable overview page by scrolling

  • See 2 to 3 groups of information if using a phone with a large screen
  • Scrolling helps users view important information together and quickly compared with swiping across cards
  • Customize the dashboard by dragging different components
Goal 2: Help users create their budget easily
Problem

Budgets is a retrospective view for spending rather than a useful tool for budgeting

  • Users need to calculate income, spending, and savings manually for budgeting

    • “I use paper and pen to calculate my spending based on income.”

  • Mint is not sufficient to help users plan ahead for the expenditure in the future

    • “I want to save money to go to New Zealand, but Mint does not have the projection function to help me.”

Before: Current Mint

Solution

Budget holistically and smartly for income, spending, and savings goal

  • Save the manual effort of calculating income, spending, and savings goal
  • Get advice on how to stay in budget
  • Get intuitive guidance from the formula staying on the top about how the budget for spending and savings goal will influence the left-over
  • Set a nice image for the goal to feel motivated and monitor the savings with the progress bar
  • Adjust the planned date in a flexible way by either inputting a number or using the slider
Goal 3: Provide users with rich and deep insights about their spending
Problem

The current data visualization cannot help meet users’ need for deep insight

  • Users want to see the comparison of the actual amount of spending in different categories

    • “A bar chart might be more helpful for me to understand exactly how much money I spent”

  • Users want to figure out the change of their spending in categories across months

    • “It would be helpful if I could see spending history under categories”

  • Users need actionable insight to do better

    • “I wish I could get actionable items beyond the data to tell me what I should do to save money”

Before: Current Mint

>
Solution

Compare the spending across months and categories

  • View the trend via line chart, which can interact with the rest information on the page
  • View the spending via the bar chart, which is an alternative view of the donut chart on overview page
  • Receive actionable and friendly advice on how to control spending
  • Drill down to spending in a category to view the trend and transaction
Goal 4: help users reduce impulse purchase
Problem

Impulse buying is a pain for saving money.

  • Users tend to pay attention to whether there is abnormal spending that indicates impulse purchases when reviewing transactions.
  • However, they tend to forget their impulse purchase when they go shopping.
Solution

Mark an impulse purchase as a “regret” and view a report to reflect on spending

  • Swipe left on a transaction to mark it easily as regret
  • Reflect on how much is spent emotionally with the report of money regrets
  • Analyze where moeny tend to be spent on impulse by viewing money regrets based on category

Design Iteration

Budgets: Make the formula easier to understand

V1 🙁

  • “Spending” and “goal” is confusing
  • The negative number is hard to interpret
  • The page looks cluttered and lacks hierarchy

V2 😃

  • Change "goal“ to "savings goal"
  • Use an actual formula to show the calculation
  • Make overall budget a separate section

Money Regrets: Separate the report from the transaction page

V1 🙁

  • The summary of money regrets on top of transactions makes users feel this page is only showing money regrets

V2 😃

  • Move the summary from the transaction page to a separate page
  • Breakdown money regrets based on category
Cleaner Visual Design
  • Increase the margin and reduced the height of charts
  • Separate the number from the chart to make the number more readable
