Digital Capabilities Model for Supply Networks (DCM) Data Viz and Website

A Wikipedia about digital supply chain which presents DCM with network visualization

Data Viz| Information Architecture | Interaction Design

Introduction

I worked as the only designer to design the data visualization, website, and branding for Digital Capabilities Model for Supply Chain. The website has been launched for supply chain professionals to understand this concept and use this modal for the digitalization of their organization.

Screenshot of the launched website

Challenge

DCM has 6 key capabilities, each of which has 7-8 sub-capabilities under each, and those sub-capabilities are also related to each other.

How might we help users explore, understand, and engage with the DCM complex concept?

Design

Explore the modal in an engaging way with network data viz

Present the model from high-level concept to detailed relationship step by step.

Display the overview of the selected capabilities on the side to support users’ understanding of the concept.

Navigate through the model in a structured way with navbar and list of capabilities.

Support a clear understanding of the site and the model and quick access to a capability.

Show the capability in the network graph accordingly based on users’ selection.

Understand where the users are with breadcrumb and fixed header

Display breadcrumb to help users understand the current capability’s position in the model and navigate to the previous level.

Use fixed header to provide context for users when they are browsing details of a capability.

Information Architecture

Exploration and Iteration

Initial Ideas

2 options were proposed based on the best practice of showing relationships.

Option 1: Network Diagram

The large bubbles are L1 capabilities and the small bubbles around are L2 capabilities

Pro: Show the L1 - L2 relationship clearly. Lead users to navigate the model step by step by clicking each bubble.

Con: Line weight of the relationship is hard to distinguish when the relationship is complex.

Option 2: Chord Diagram

The outer circle segments are L1 capabilities and the inner segments are L2 connected to each other via arcs.

Pro: Clear line weight of relatinoship because the size of the arc is proportional to the importance of the flow.

Con: Potential challenge in space to visualize L1 and L2 and lack of interactivity for exploring from L1 to L2.

Prototype with Real Data

I used Amchart, a JS data-viz library, to create the 2 options with real data. Option one could better help achieve the goal of helping users understand, navigate, and explore the relationship between different capabilities.

Network Diagram

😃 Show the L1 and L2 relationship clearly. Lead users to navigate the model step by step by clicking each bubble.

😃 Look clean by only revealing the selected circle and its related circles
