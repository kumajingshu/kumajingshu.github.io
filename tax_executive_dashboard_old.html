Tax Executive Dashboard

Providing visibility of tax reporting and tax analytics for global corporations

Interaction Design

Introduction

The complexity of tax preparation is a pain for everyone. Managing the corporate tax is far more challenging for a Tax Executive of a multinational company. My client of this project would like to build a platform to bring transparency and efficiency of corporate tax reporting.

As part of this large initiative, my task focused on designing a dashboard to help the user, Tax Executive, to obtain the visibility of the status of their numerous tax obligations and gain insights into their global tax position.

For confidentiality reasons, this portfolio piece only introduces the key features and screen briefly.

My Role
  • Created whiteboard sketches to communicate design ideas with designers and manager
  • Created wireframes, data visualization, hi-fi mock-ups, and prototypes and iterated on an agile fashion based on the feedback from stakeholders
  • Synthesized previous research and analyzed existing systems; summarized and presented key features and user journey to stakeholders to get alignment and priorities
Tool
  • Sketch
  • Invision

User & Challenge

Debra is a busy Tax VP in a multinational corporation, which owns branches all over the US and the world. Each branch needs to pay the state tax or tax in another country, which makes tracking the progress of the tax preparation in each state and understanding the global tax payment challenging.

  • How might we help Debra identify problems during the process of the tax preparation as soon as possible to avoid missing a due date?
  • How might we provide clear data visualizations to get insight into tax payment?

Design Overview

The design aims for helping the busy Tax Executive identify the reason for a delay during tax preparation in one view.

Overview - Pie Chart

Give the user a quick overview of all obligations based on due date

Outstanding Items - KPI

Prompt users the most urgent problems

Progress in each stage

Help the user better understand the where the problem in the KPI is and identify off-track items in the early stage in order to avoid missing the final due

Tax Analytics

Provide insights about the comparison between 2 years and between the actual and statutory rate with bar charts

Take-aways

  • Build the persona
    Within a short timeframe and limited access to real users, I gained empathy and understanding of the users by having close communication with Tax SME and friends who work as tax accountant, and digesting existing user research.
  • Use design as a tool of communication
    There were multiple stakeholders involved and it was hard to get feedback and alignment. Showing the design and iterating quickly was the most effective way to get their thoughts and provide a start point for them to talk to each other.
  • Use design system
    I followed the design system developed for the large project where this dashboard sits, which ensured the consistency of the look and feel and increased my working efficiency.
