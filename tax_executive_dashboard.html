Tax Executive Dashboard

Providing visibility of tax reporting and tax analytics for global corporations

Interaction Design

Introduction

The client of this project would like to build a POC for a Tax Executive Dashboard as part of their initiative to reimagine and demonstrate the digital tax experience.

Within 1.5 months, I delivered the hi-fi mockups and prototypes. Starting from ambiguous requirements, I understood the problems and process, analyzed multiple tax systems, and identified the key features. I worked closely with the manager, stakeholders, and SME, and got alignments across these different parties.

For confidentiality reasons, this portfolio piece only introduces the key features and screen briefly.

Tool
  • Sketch
  • Invision

The Challenge

The complexity of tax preparation is a pain for everyone. Managing the corporate tax is far more challenging for a Tax Executive of a multinational company. How can we reduce the complexity of overseeing the corporate tax for a busy Tax Executive?

Solution Overview

  • Overview

    • Get a quick overview of all obligations based on due date

  • Outstanding Items

    • Keep updated about the most urgent problems with KPI

  • Issue Identification in Workflow

    • Identify problems in early stage to avoid missing the final due

  • Tax Analytics

    • Gain insights with intuitive data visualization

Approach

I worked closely with the manager, stakeholders, and SME to gather their requirments and feedback, understand the problem and process, and get their alignments.

Meet the Persona

Debra is a busy Tax VP in a multinational corporation which needs to pay tax over the states and the world. Tracking the progress of numerous tax preparation is always challenging for her.

Needs
  • Gain a high-level and clear overview of the real-time status and issues as an executive
  • Identify the issues during tax preparation timely so that no due date will be missed.
  • Understand the tax payment across different regions and its change in the recent 2 years
Behavior
  • View the tax obligations based on the due date
  • View the tax preparation in their workflow, which includes 4 steps: data collection, preparation, review, and complete

Design

How can we help Debra identify problems during the process of the tax preparation as soon as possible to avoid missing a due date?
Compliance Status

Enable users to connect the problem and the reason in one view efficiently with the interaction between KPI and workflow chart

KPI

  • Provide users a high-level view about the most urgent issues
  • Work as a filter to check the step of the issue

Workflow Bar Chart

  • A medium to connect overview and details
  • Show the aggregate view of obligations in each steps
  • Help users solve problems in early step to avoid overdue
  • Drill down to details of obligations
How can we help Debra understand the status of the tax compliance and gain insight with clear data visualization?
Summary

Show an overview of the compliance based on the due date with a donut chart

Donut chart

  • Better use the space
  • Better show the concept of breakdown and avoid the confusion of the total caused by the segmented bar chart in the original design

Toggle between obligation and data requests

  • Obligation is the default view, because filing a tax return is a higher level and more important task than collecting data for the persona
  • Reduce the visual noise

Layout

  • The position of the top left corner makes the overview the first information to be read
Tax Analytics

Explain how complex concept with easy-to-digest data visualization

Purpose

  • Compare the tax rate of this year with the previous year
  • Compare the tax rate and statutory tax rate in each year
  • It might be problematic when a tax rate increase a lot or when it is much higher than the statutory rate

Bar

  • Show the tax return in 2 years and the trend

Line

  • Show the statutory tax rate in each year
  • Statutory tax rate works as a target in each year and fluctuates less across years

Iteration

V1: Show details of the problem in a table

  • Pro
    Show both high-level and the exact problems in one view
  • Con
    Not effective in some edge cases, eg. when there is 0 overview or when there are many problematic obligations

V1: Table View

V2: Present items in a workflow as a middle layer

  • Pro
    Show a whole picture of obligations in each stages; bring connectivity to the existing information in obligation tab
  • Con
    Need to drill down to another page to show details (viewing details is not a priority for target user)

V2: Chart View - Chevron

V3: Simplify the visual design of the visualization

  • From Chevron to Timeline

    • V3: Chart View - Timeline

Take-aways

  • Analyze complex systems and process

    • I used diverse design artifacts to synthesize large amount of information I got. For example, I used journey maps to summarize the steps, pains, and opportunities. I also created interaction maps to understand different tax systems I learned. To get a quick understanding of the process of tax reporting, I took the initiative to interview my friend, who is a tax associate, in addition to talking with the SME.

  • Use design as a tool of communication

    • There were multiple stakeholders involved and it was hard to get feedback and alignment. Showing the design and iterating quickly was the most effective way to get their thoughts and provide a start point for them to talk to each other.

  • Use design system

    • I followed the design system developed for the large project where this dashboard sits, which ensured the consistency of the look and feel and increased my working efficiency.
