Amesite

Making core courses more engaging for underclassmen

Mobile App | Product Design | Interaction Design | UX Research

Intro

Amesite is an AI software start-up company targeting college course market. In the semester of winter 2018, our team in UMSI Design Clinic designed the first prototype for Amesite to achieve its mission, which is to deliver cost-effective cloud-based digital versions of lower-level courses that will greatly enhance the learning experience of students.

My Role

I worked as the team lead in a team of 5 to lead the research and design, and manage the project and the communication with the client.

  • Adopted methodologies such as affinity grouping and multivoting for brainstorming, and parallel design in the design phase.
  • Created wireframes, hi-fi prototype and style guide, led design review sessions, and crafted the final deliverables.
Tool
  • Sketch

Challenge

The physical class is not attractive. When freshmen are taking fundamental courses such as Psychology 101, it is very hard for them to stay focused in the classroom with over 100 students. The current online classes cannot compare with the experience of Spotify, Pinterest, or Youtube which match large amount of information to people's taste using AI.

With this insight from her 17-year experience as a professor, our client believes that college can be more engaging using artificial intelligence. Thus, she asked us to explore:

How might we make the experience of fundamental course engaging and customized online by integrating the sticky components of popular sites & apps?

Approach

We adopted the user-centered design. The users and client are involved in every stage of the design process.

Solution Overview

Amesite personalizes the learning experience by providing underclassmen with a social environment where they can consume curated content and interact directly with their course material and peers on their smartphones.

Learn from curated content and peers' projects

Interact with peers while watching course video

Get feedback to make progress

Research

What makes a platform sticky?

Based on the comparative analysis of over 30 sticky platforms including social networking sites, game, and fitness apps, we identified 4 sticky elements:

How do target users study, work, relax, and succeed with these elements?

We further interviewed 12 target users and collected 57 survey responses to explore these elements in students' lives.

  • Goal

    • They divide their goal into subtasks and like using google calendar to help them schedule and manage time.

  • Social

    • They like forming study groups.

    They value the recommendations, experience, and feedback from peers.

    They take pride in showing / teaching / influencing others.

  • Story

    • Social media such as Facebook and Instagram is an important channel for them to get news and view stories of friends and others.

  • Feedback

    • In terms of study, both qualitative and quantitative feedback is important. The comparative position is important in quantitative feedback.

Ideation

Brainstorming, grouping, and voting
  • Step 1: Brainstormed 30 ideas based on the design opportunities found from research.
  • Step 2: Grouped the ideas into themes such as goal, social interaction, social media, story, feedback, and reputation.
  • Step 3: Voted for the ideas based on the importance and creativity and picked the idea that got the most votes.
  • Step 4: Reviewed the ideas with the client and made decisions about key features.
Sketching

When we sketching ideas, at least 2 members were in charge of sketching one feature which provided design alternatives for us to choose the best design option.

Design

Newsfeeds: learn from curated content and peers

Both traditional learning and online learning are usually not as interesting as Youtube or Instagram which recommend users what they are interested in and connect them with more people. How might we make learning more personalized and social?

The homepage is designed with dynamic contents including relevant news articles of users' course and interest, and Q&A and projects from classmates. These contents will help students learn anytime and anywhere, like how they read stories and use social networking sites.

Design Rationale
  • We designed a banner with a bright color to prompt users rather than using much space for the video thumbnails or course list, because although watching course videos is an important user task, those contents are usually static and hard to increase users' activities on the App.
  • Bigger and bolder titles are designed to make the homepage content focused. Different from the general SNS, profile picture or name of students was less emphasized.
  • The section to explain why users are seeing a content is provided in order to resolve the confusion users had about the newsfeeds in the usability tests.
Course video: learn in an interactive classroom

Sitting in a large classroom is boring, but watching course video online is lonely. Students like learning in a group with their peers. How might we enhance the sense of belonging and create a social experience while watching a course video?

The course video shows users who are watching with them now and their classmates' real-time reaction. Users can also interact with the instructors and classmates in the Q&A section.

Design Rationale
  • The page is divided by 2 tabs, introduction and Q&A, to make the structure clear and navigate users to the discussion with peers easily.
  • The real-time reactions are in the form of emoji rather than text which may cause online harassment easily and interrupt the learning experience.
  • The users can be anonymous in the Q&A section to encourage students to ask questions and avoid harassment.
Task & Achievement: motivate students to succeed

What makes people keep working on something? Students break down their goal into subtasks and use google calendar to schedule their tasks. When they finish a big project, they take pride in showing their works. They collect badges when playing games. They record data to help them work out better. How might we integrate these elements into learning?

To support task management, 2 views, a to-do list and a calendar, are designed for users to focus on the most important and recent taks and plan ahead for a longer period of time.

Users will feel the sense of achievement by sharing the projects they are proud of, such as their final paper, and collecting badges for being active, such as answering questions from peers. They can also understand themselves from the dashboard, which will be designed further in the next step.

Design Rationale
  • Cards are used to organize tasks to make the interface clear with space between similar information.
  • Cards are used for the projects to organize the project information effectively and delicately and make what users are proud of stand out from the page.
  • On "me" page, 4 tabs are used to segment information, activity, and data about a user effectively. The number is designed to be big and bold to emphasize a user' achievement.
Course: organize learning materials with ease

Getting access to the course materials and grade is the basic function for a learning App but the information architecture is complicated. How might we present a large amount of information about the course clearly?

Design Rationale
  • Cards are used for content about course and grades to avoid the overcrowdedness caused by too much similar information on the page, which helps user scan information.
  • "X" (close) rather than "<" (back) is used to close the page directed by an item in a list, such as the course material from a part of a weekly course, the grade of a week.

Impact

Following the project's completion in mid-April, Amesite announced on June 14 the closing of a $5.5 million private placement financing, including approximately $1 million from insiders, and the completion of a go-public transaction through a reverse merger with Lola One Acquisition Corporation (“Lola One”). The development and commercialization of Amesite will make college education more fun, more accessible, and more cost-effective.

Take-aways

  • Think from a different angle

    • Based on the research, laptop is actually the major device for students to learn, but smart phone is the device used most frequently. This inspired us that we should try to find opportunities on the device students spend their most time on.

  • Work as a team lead

    • I led the team to create high-quality deliverables through multiple iterations in a busy semester by making and executing the plan, adopting design methodologies to enhance the efficiency of decision making, and asking the why behind the design.

  • Get the client involved

    • We collaborated with our client very closely. Our client gave us feedback not only from the business side, but also as a professor who has observations and thinkings about education, and a mother who understands the life of the young generation.
